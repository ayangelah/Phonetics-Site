UPSID (UCLA Phonological Segment Inventory Database)

Data on the phonological systems of 451 languages, with programs to access it, by Ian Maddieson and Kristin Precoda. This is an elderly DOS program (Windows only), neither of whose developers are still at UCLA, and no support is offered. Available to download as a single zip file. A web interface

Corpora Available outside of UCLA

Buckeye

The Buckeye Corpus of conversational speech contains high-quality recordings from 40 speakers in Columbus OH conversing freely with an interviewer. The speech has been orthographically transcribed and phonetically labeled.

Last updated 2005
CORAAL

The Corpus of Regional African American Language (CORAAL) is the first public corpus of AAL data. CORAAL features recorded speech from regional varieties of AAL and includes the audio recordings along with time-aligned orthographic transcription from over 220 sociolinguistic interviews from speakers born between 1888 and 2005.

Updated regularly
TIMIT

The TIMIT corpus of read speech is designed to provide speech data for acoustic-phonetic studies and for the development and evaluation of automatic speech recognition systems. TIMIT contains broadband recordings of 630 speakers of eight major dialects of American English, each reading ten phonetically rich sentences.

Last updated 1993
BU Radio Speech Corpus

The Boston University Radio Speech Corpus was collected primarily to support research in text-to-speech synthesis, particularly generation of prosodic patterns. The corpus consists of professionally read radio news data, including speech and accompanying annotations, suitable for speech and language research.

Last updated 1997
